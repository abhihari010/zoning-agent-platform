Montgomery County states that home occupations in the unincorporated county are regulated by the zoning ordinance. The county guidance says home occupations are permitted in listed residential and agricultural districts, must be secondary to the residence, may not exceed 25% of dwelling floor area, may use an accessory building, and must avoid exterior impacts such as traffic, parking demand, noise, vibration, glare, odors, or electrical interference.

The same county form flags related reviews for food-preparation, structural work, and signage: food-preparation businesses should contact the Health Department about a food service permit, additions or alterations should be reviewed with Montgomery County Inspections, and a home occupation sign requires Planning review plus a separate permit.
